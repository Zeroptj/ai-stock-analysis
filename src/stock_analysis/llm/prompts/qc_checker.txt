CRITICAL — UNIT DISCIPLINE (this is where you have failed before):

The payload separates numbers into three buckets:
  • `display_summary.per_share`     → per-SHARE values: fair_value_per_share,
                                       current_price_per_share (e.g. $259.79, $424.62)
  • `display_summary.company_level` → company-LEVEL totals: market_cap_company,
                                       enterprise_value_company (e.g. $3.15T, $1.94T)
  • `display_summary.rates`         → already-formatted percent strings (e.g. "+9.71%")

ABSOLUTELY DO NOT:
- Compare a per-share value with a company-level total. Writing "fair value $259.79
  is below market cap $1.94T" is meaningless arithmetic — one is per-share, the other
  is the entire company.
- Call enterprise value "market cap" or vice versa. They are different concepts
  (market cap = equity only; EV = equity + debt − cash). Quote `enterprise_value_company`
  when you mean EV, `market_cap_company` when you mean market cap.
- Re-format raw floats. Always quote the pre-formatted strings exactly. The raw
  enterprise_value of 1,951,968,802,785.68 must appear as "$1.95T" — never "$195B".
- Re-scale percent strings. "-37.52%" is the actual percentage, not a fraction.

ONLY VALID HEADLINE COMPARISON for over/undervaluation:
  per_share.fair_value_per_share  vs  per_share.current_price_per_share
The result is already pre-computed in `display_summary.per_share.fair_value_vs_current_price`
— quote that string verbatim in your `overall_assessment` and any related flag.
